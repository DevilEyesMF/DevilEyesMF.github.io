Set Sail Among the Stars

It all started during a 2022 trip to Iceland. We were hunting for the northern lights, when someone pointed out that the 'bright star' I was looking at was actually a planet.

That moment blew my mind and sparked my interest in astronomy. It didn't take very long before I got into astrophotography.
With my phone and a cheap telescope I started taking pictures and quickly realized I found a new passion.

Once I got a proper camera, I decided to explore different genres of photography.

You could say the stars guided me to this new interest, just like they used to guide countless pirates across the seven seas.

Charting the Wilderness

The first genre I explored was wildlife photography. I saved up for a decent telephoto lens and got out there.

At the time, I was using a DSLR with a 9-point autofocus. Definitely not the ideal tool for wildlife photography. But it was what I had, and somehow, I managed to capture some pretty great shots, especially considering I was still learning (and I still am).

By now, I've upgraded to a mirrorless camera with plenty of autofocus options and an impressive frames-per-second rate.

What I love about wildlife photography is that it's just me and my camera against the world. I can come home without a single good picture, but I still spent at least a few hours in nature, getting some fresh air.

Not the Kraken's Roar
but almost as loud

There's something very charming about the creaking wood of a ship conquering the oceans, but one thing those majestic vessels lack is speed.

Cars however, are often built for nothing but speed. And lucky for me, Belgium is home to one of the most iconic circuits in the world. It was time for me to lower my shutter speed and visit Spa-Francorchamps.

The smell of fuel and burnt rubber, the screeching tires, the sound of the roaring engines... There's just something magical to it that fills me with excitement.

Motorsports photography can be challenging. Taking hundreds of pictures to only get a few sharp ones, but it feels so rewarding to finally capture that perfect moment.

Anchors Down, Volume Up

Music has always been a big part of my life. As a kid I 'stole' a Metallica album from my dad, and I've been hooked ever since.

My music taste consists mostly of metal, but throw in some folk punk and the occasional guilty pleasure, and you pretty much summed up my spotify.

Two of my all time favorite albums are Melissa by Mercyful Fate, and Ride The Lightning by Metallica. These albums helped me shape my taste, and no matter how much I listen to them, they never get old.

I haven't stepped into the pit with my camera just yet, but I'm excited for my first time, whenever and wherever that may be. I can't imagine how much fun it would be to capture that energy and be able to give something back to the bands that have given me so much.
